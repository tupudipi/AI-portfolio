Excited about the capabilities of DALL·E 3, I wanted to have some fun with my friends and put everyone's cars into the video game Need for Speed Most Wanted 2005. This required detailed prompts, with granular, hyper specific and focused cues and instructions. The journey was as follows:

I first set out to generate my own car inside the video game world. For this, I used the prompt: "Hi! Could you please show me some images of a grey citroen c4 2007 hatchback model, in the style of NFS Most Wanted 2005?". This did generate a grey Citroen that resembled my car, but it wasn't quite right, and the backdrop was not what I imagined:

And so I tried to hone in the specific cues necessary to produce the backdrop I was imagining, that fit the specific game world I was trying to imitate:"HHmm, this looks cool but the atmosphere doesn't quite look like NFS Most Wanted 2005. to start, the game only takes place during daytime, and is very well known for its autumnal atmosphere, with falling leaves and warm sunset light, plus the gritty "worn" and "lived-in" look of Rockport city, with it's cracked pavement and grafitti.". And the output for this prompt was much better. The style of the image was right:

Then I added police cars to better suit the action of the video game: "Wow, this looks amazing! Could you please try to add some police cars chasing the citroen?"

And finally I resolved the issue of the car model being newer than my own real life car, and also added the actual license plate of my car: "Wow, these are awesome! the citroen looks kinda new though, to be honest. could you please make it be the actual 2004 model? also, i hope i'm not asking for too much, but could you please make the number plate be DB-07-MVD". The image produced fit what I had set out to generate perfectly, capturing both the vibe and action of the video game world, and the likeness of my real car:

After generating images of my car in the video game world, I then modified the prompts to generate each of my friends' cars.
